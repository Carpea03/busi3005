The Three Stages

Structure

Each stage releases new information on Canvas at a fixed time. You cannot see later stages until they open. This simulates how strategy consultants process information progressively.

Stage 1
Initial Analysis
Background context about two organisations. Apply Constraint Mapping to analyse their AI implementation approaches.
40 minutes · 500–600 words
Stage 2
Problem Response
New challenges emerge. Apply the Strategy Evaluation Matrix to reassess both organisations. Show how your thinking evolves.
40 minutes · 500–600 words
Stage 3
Final Synthesis
Stakeholder responses and complications. Apply Failure Analysis and synthesise all three stages into strategic recommendations.
40 minutes · 500–800 words

Key: Your Stage 1 analysis doesn't need to be "right" — it needs to reflect genuine thinking. Stage 2 and 3 reward students who adapt, not students who hedge.

What You Submit — Each Stage

Required

Each stage requires two submissions to the corresponding Canvas assignment:

📄 Analysis
Your written analysis (500–600 words for Stages 1 & 2; 500–800 words for Stage 3).

Format: Text entry in Canvas, Word document, or PDF — your choice.

Apply the relevant framework. Take a position. Show your reasoning.
📋 Prompt Log
All prompts used — numbered, with brief notes on why you refined or changed them.

Format: Text entry in Canvas, Word document, or PDF — your choice.

Copy and paste your prompts and key AI outputs directly. Failed prompts are valuable evidence.

The submission portal for each stage closes permanently when the next stage opens. If you have not submitted before it closes, you cannot submit for that stage. Leave at least 2–3 minutes for uploading.

How You're Being Assessed

Criteria
30%
Progressive AI Collaboration
Quality and sophistication of your AI collaboration — not just quantity of prompts. Show purposeful refinement, critical evaluation of outputs, and strategic tool selection across stages. Which AI for which task?
30%
Organisational Context Analysis
Depth and nuance in analysing how context shapes AI implementation. Show your analysis evolving across stages as new information emerges. Don't just list constraints — analyse their implications.
25%
Adaptive Thinking
How effectively you incorporate new and contradictory information. Demonstrate strategic pivots, not just additive updates. Stage 2 should challenge your Stage 1 — embrace that.
15%
Synthesis Quality
Final integration of all stages into a coherent narrative with clear, defensible recommendations. Take a position and justify it — don't hedge everything.

Rules & Expectations

Assessment Conditions
You Must
  • Work individually — no discussion with other students during the assessment
  • Submit both items (analysis + prompt log) for each stage before the portal closes
  • Document all AI interactions in your prompt log as you work — copy and paste prompts and key outputs
You May
  • Use any AI tools — ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Copilot, Gemini, Grok, and others
  • Use multiple tools simultaneously — different tools suit different tasks
  • Access the internet for research
  • Reference your notes and course materials

Each workshop session has a different case study. The Wednesday and Friday scenarios are not the same. There is no benefit to sharing content between sessions.

Do not share any case study content with anyone outside this room. Do not discuss your analysis during the assessment.

Strategic Advice

From Your Instructor
🔧
Use Your Full Toolkit
You've spent 3 weeks building capabilities across text AI, research agents, multimodal tools, and workflow thinking. This assessment rewards students who draw on that range strategically.
🧠
Your Judgment Is the Differentiator
AI can research, structure, and draft. But your judgment about what matters, what's missing, and what to recommend separates excellent from adequate.
⚡
Engage With Complexity
There are no clean answers. Taking a defensible position and justifying it is far more valuable than hedging everything. Don't simplify away the tension.
📝
Read the Details Carefully
Details in the briefings matter. Information that seems minor in Stage 1 may become critical in Stage 3. Read carefully before you start prompting.

Remember: 5 excellent, iterative prompts showing critical refinement are worth more than 20 basic queries. Document your failures — they demonstrate learning.
