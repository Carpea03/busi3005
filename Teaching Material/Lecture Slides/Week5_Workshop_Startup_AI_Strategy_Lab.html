Framework Refresher
Designing Your MVAIP
Your Minimum Viable AI Product answers one question: Can AI solve this customer's problem well enough that they'll use it?

1. Define One Core Function

Not five features — one. What is the single thing your AI does that makes someone say "I need this"?

2. Select Free Tools

ChatGPT/Claude API (free tiers), Lovable/Bolt.new (prototyping), Google Forms (data capture), Notion (workflow).

3. Design the Validation Test

How do you prove people want this before building anything complex? What's your Build-Measure-Learn first cycle?

⚠️ The #1 Mistake

Designing your MVAIP to impress your instructor instead of solve a customer problem.

Your pitch is assessed on strategic thinking and feasibility, not on how many AI features you can cram in.

Think Grammarly's first version, not Humane's AI Pin.

Making the Most of Instructor Time
Questions to Ask During This Session
The instructor is circulating. Don't wait — flag them down. Here are the highest-value questions to ask:

"Is our scope right?"

Too ambitious = unfeasible pitch. Too narrow = underwhelming pitch. Get this calibrated now.

"Does this avoid the wrapper trap?"

If OpenAI could add your feature tomorrow, your concept has no moat. Test this early.

"What's our biggest blind spot?"

An outside perspective catches what group-think misses. The answer may surprise you.

"Can we actually build this with free tools?"

Feasibility is a core assessment criterion. Get a reality check on your technical plan.

Speed Pitching
The 90-Second Elevator Pitch
Startup founders pitch in lifts, corridors, and coffee queues. You have 90 seconds to hook your audience. Here's the structure:

1

The Problem

~15 seconds
What painful, specific problem exists?

2

Your Solution

~20 seconds
How does your AI product solve it?

3

Why AI

~20 seconds
Why must this be AI-powered?

4

Why Now

~15 seconds
What's changed to make this possible?

5

The Ask

~20 seconds
What do you need? End with clarity.

20
Speed Pitching
Giving Feedback Like an Investor
After each group pitches, the audience provides structured feedback. Be constructive, specific, and honest.

✓ Strengths

What was compelling? Did the problem feel real? Was the AI use case clear? Would you use this product?

⚡ Sharpen

Where did the pitch lose you? What questions weren't answered? Was any part confusing or vague?

🚩 Red Flags

Does this smell like a wrapper? Is the moat clear? Could ChatGPT do this already? Are the unit economics viable?

💡 Suggestions

What pivot or refinement would make this stronger? How could they sharpen the moat or narrow the focus?

What We Learned Today
✓

Success comes from problem-first thinking

Grammarly and Canva both started by solving a painful, specific problem. The AI came later.

✗

Failure follows predictable patterns

Technology Mirage, Open Source Guillotine, Burn Rate Bomb. The AI Pin hit all three. Now you can spot these early.

⚡

Constraints are your competitive advantage

Free tools + lean thinking forces strategic clarity. The "limitation" of Assignment 2 is actually its strength.

🏰

Your moat is never the AI model itself

In 2026, wrappers are dying. Your advantage is proprietary data, deep workflow integration, brand trust, or unique distribution.
